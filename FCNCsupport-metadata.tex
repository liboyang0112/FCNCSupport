\AtlasAbstract{%
A search is presented for flavor-changing neutral current $tHq$ interactions with $H\to\taup\taum$ using a data set collected with the ATLAS detector at the LHC, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of $139$~fb$^{-1}$ of proton-proton collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 13~TeV. The search targets top quark pair production with one top decaying to Wb and the other to Hq, and single $qg \to tH$($q=c/u$) single top production in association with a Higgs boson. All W-boson decay modes are considered, while the Higgs boson is assumed to decay to a $\taup\taum$ pair. Leptonic($\tau \rightarrow \ell v_{\ell} v_{\tau}$) and hadronic($\tau \rightarrow hardrons v_{\tau}$) decays of the $\tau$ lepton are considered.  No excess is found and upper limits are set on the equivalent $t\to uH$ and $t\to cH$ decay branching ratios. The observed 95\% confidence level bounds are $\mathcal{B}(t\to uH) <0.072\%$ and $\mathcal{B}(t\to cH) < 0.099\%$, while the expected limits are $\mathcal{B}(t\to uH) < 0.036^{+0.017}_{-0.010}\%$ and $\mathcal{B}(t\to cH) < 0.050^{+0.022}_{-0.014}\%$, respectively
%%The search is performed in the decay chain $t\bar{t} \to Wb+Hq$ or $qg \to tH \to Wb+H$ ($q=c/u$), where the $W$ boson decays inclusively and H decays to $\taup\taum$.
